Time Management App

Timely Mobile App

Project Goal

Create a time management app that allows users to effortlessly track their daily activities, where users can leave insightful comments after each session, providing context for deviations from typical durations, The app processes this data to generate informative visual trends, helping users identify patterns and optimize their routines, and allowing them to perform accurate time estimates later on.

Research

I used secondary research sources to gather user information in order to understand the processes and emotions, common behaviors, needs and frustrations around the problem Timely was trying to solve. The information was gathered from productivity/time management forums, social accounts and other productivity/time management app reviews. I compiled comments both positive and negative based on the user's social position and age. Finally, I put together two different user personas and a user journey map

User Persona

Jonas Maximilian

Medical student with part-time job, 20

Lives in an urban city with his family

Jonas is an ambitious and driven medical student with a strong desire to excel in his studies and future medical career. He leads a busy life as he juggles rigorous medical courses with a part-time job to support his education expenses. Jonas understands the importance of time management to balance his academic commitments, work responsibilities, and personal life.

"I have to juggle a multitude of difficult courses while having a part-time job, so I definitely need to manage my time efficiently. "

Goals

  • Efficiently manage his time to succeed in his academic pursuits
  • Maintain excellent grades in medical school while performing well at his job to secure financial stability
  • Organize his schedule effectively, performing accurate time estimates to maintain a healthy work-life balance

Frustrations

  • It’s really hard to keep track of how long do he takes to perform an activity, hence inaccurate time estimates
  • Scheduling his day takes a lot of time
  • He doesn’t get to finish my todo list often

Needs and Desires

Jonas is seeking a time management application that can streamline his daily activities. He desires a tool that can help him allocate study hours efficiently, track the time spent on various tasks, and provide valuable insights to optimize his productivity. As a busy student with limited time, Jonas is looking for an app that integrates with his existing calendar and offers seamless time estimates for activities.

Martha Green

Lawyer studying for the bar exam, 28

Lives in a busy city with her husband and her son

Martha Green is a determined and multitasking individual who recently became a mom. She graduated from law school 2 years ago and is now working towards taking the bar exam to fulfill her dream of becoming a licensed attorney. Martha leads a demanding life that requires efficient time management to balance her family responsibilities, legal work, and bar exam preparation.

"Taking care of my family, working as a lawyer, and studying for the bar exam, I must organize my life, or it'll be a total mess."

Goals

  • Be more productive
  • Excel in her legal career and successfully pass the bar exam
  • Organize her schedule effectively, so that she can allocate more time to spend with her family

Frustrations

  • Handling diverse demands in her life makes it even harder to keep track of how long she spend on each activity
  • Missing some events on her schedule due to wrong time estimates
  • Not spending enough time with her family

Needs and Desires

Martha is looking for a productivity application that she can use to track her daily activities and provide feedback on each task. She desires an app that can process this data and offer valuable insights on how to improve her productivity. Martha also seeks a scheduling feature that automatically allocates the necessary time for each activity, including appropriate margins, eliminating the need for her to calculate the duration of each event. She wants a comprehensive and user-friendly tool that streamlines time allocation.

User Journey

Ideation

Ideation phase started based on the findings from the research phase to generate solutions that would eventually lead to a testable wireframe prototype. Market standard methods was used to come to the conclusions that defined the first designs of the product.

Problem Statements

  • Jonas Maximilian is an aspiring medical student who seeks an effective time management app to enhance his productivity and better manage his demanding academic schedule
  • Martha Green is a working lawyer and new mother preparing for the bar exam who requires a reliable scheduling solution that provides accurate time estimates because she aims to organize her day efficiently, balancing her professional and personal commitments to avoid chaos

Hypothesis Statements

  • By providing users with a visual representation of their activity trends over time, we expect that users will be more conscious of their time management habits and make proactive changes to optimize their routines
  • By offering seamless integration with Google Calendar and providing accurate time estimates, we assume that users will experience reduced scheduling conflicts and better adherence to planned activities
  • By allowing users to leave comments explaining deviations in activity durations, we expect that users will gain insights into external factors influencing their time management, leading to improved planning and decision-making
  • Through user-friendly and customizable interfaces for activity tracking, we assume that users will find it easier to log their activities regularly, resulting in a more accurate representation of their daily routines

Competitive Audit

Goal Statement

This app will empower users to optimize their time, enhance productivity, and achieve a balanced work-life routine through seamless activity tracking, insightful time analysis, and intuitive integration with Google Calendar.

UX Research

With the wireframe prototype ready for testing, I contacted five different acquaintances who would help me by giving feedback based on their personal experience using it.

Research Goals

The research aims to enhance the app's user experience by identifying opportunities to optimize user flow, boost user retention, increase new user acquisition, and address user frustrations. Through usability testing and qualitative analysis, the study seeks to streamline navigation, improve engagement and onboarding, and alleviate pain points, ultimately creating an intuitive and engaging application that positively impacts both user satisfaction and growth metrics.

Usability Study

In order to test the app, I came up with four different prompts or tasks the users should try to perform without any feedback: Prompt 1: From the home screen, create a new activity Prompt 2: Add a task to the activity you created and a start a session Prompt 3: Pause the session and return to the home page Prompt 4: Return back to the session screen and resume the session Prompt 5: Change your preference settings Prompt 6: Describe your experience with the app Prompt 7: What are you biggest Frustration Prompt 8: What did standout for you the most? The users shared their screen on a videocall, so I was able to see their click path and ask for their feedback once the tasks were performed. These are 2 summaries of the five test sessions:

Patterns & Insight Identification

These are some of the most significant conclusions I came by after testing the prototype: 3 out of 5 participants had hard times figuring out what is the difference between an activity and a task 1 out of 5 couldn’t figure out how to change the preferences settings 3 out of 5 stated that the app can be used in other ways than trying to be productive like during working out and cooking

Affinity Diagram

With the information gathered from the test, I built an affinity diagram to find other common themes and pain points I could work on to improve the app's experience design.
